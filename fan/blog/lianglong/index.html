YELVREKHARON AISER

yelvrekháron áiser

The Tale of Two Brothers

An epic from the far-flung land of Aseron, about two dragon gods of rain, their rise and clash with Asyr Shai. These gods are said to control the rains and make the land habitable and temperate. However, since they are separated, their powers are weaker, and that is why droughts and storms still occasionally scour the land.

Draconic script is included. In the east, Draconic was once written in seal-script, like all other scripts, but later an alphabet spread from the west, that was adopted for use. Eastern Dragonkind often still prefers to use Seal Script for formal or decorative occasions.

Of grey sun-scorched earth, / zon dir asiir yirins
And of Xiron's grace, / A zon xironon krars
Born from mortal hearth, / zon esxin siak
Of the draconic race. / a zon idiks rekhar

Two brothers were born, / Ahr yelvor genisins,
In great mountains deep, / Ith hakros vath,
Eyes of burning dawn, / Arum san asai yirin,
Scales of azure sea. / Zaakos san xazathur.

On the sun-scorched earth, / Zon dir asiir yirins,
The flood-rains came fast, / Miirvuthos xyae komins,
Bringing with them death, / Nal kad komins khars,
And famine as they passed. / A likhres tao yirins.

People starved, people died, / Arik likhains, arik kharatins,
In this chaotic world, / Ith hir kanai diskore,
The twins watched their plight, / Ahyor danins kadon kastais,
The chaos they try withstand. / Kans diskora ankonins.

But from Xiron’s grace, / Ir zon xironon krars,
Rose divinity of Gods, / Gaimins xana tanie,
Magic interlaced, / Makara sinyarvins,
Within the brothers’ blood. / Yelvoron arkhai tha siin.

They watched in distress, / Ahyor nal v’riae danins,
As chaos awashed the land, / Kai diskora dir pela’ins,
And with magic they possessed, / A nal makara kad yarins,
To guard them, they planned. / Arik, kad sis kantayin.

They raised heads and roared, / Kad gaimins, vrakhains,
A command to the sky, / Tarxaal tao sara,
To the rains that lashed and poured, / Tao miirvuthos vixrins,
To fall gently from high. / Sii ariae, a ervaie.

And the people rejoiced, / A arik karomins,
Blessed with gentle rains, / Vuthos ervaie gardin,
But the prayers they voiced, / Ir gada kad lirins,
Met another’s disdain. / Allaron araks saerins.

The wrath of Asyr Shai, / Asiiron khimer kharase,
Was terror to behold, / Siins san diizis vaivere,
All shall quake in fear, / Enad has xir’rakhiin,
Afore the goddess of gold! / Dimur xanakhan kinae!

How dare they half blood, / Khak tamiin yamarkhai,
How dare they half divine, / Khak tamiin yaxana,
Taint the world with mud, / Diathiin kharon kanai,
Scorn that of her design? / Parakin kharon xaeda?

But torment cannot come, / Ir kad dkhat si varantin,
To those divine within, / Tao kad nal xana ith,
Though in her eyes scum, / Akai tao khar daet siin,
They were still of kin. / Kad siins atane kath.

So another sentence dealt, / Allar timorik si maizin,
To be torn asunder, / Si skazin khiyaris’,
In Damak Dai, one dwelt, / Ith damak dai yir xirin,
One exiled far yonder. / Yir si aeksirin makris’

So a balance rose, / Ithar iseryi gaimins,
Between the earth and sky, / Itharer dir a sara,
Where rains gave repose, / Ton vuthos yaan malin,
From the chaos of Shai. / Zon xaion diskora.
