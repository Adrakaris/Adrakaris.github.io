I featured in an episode on my friend Leo's podcast, YASP. You can listen to it here, or on Google Podcasts and Spotify. It's called Insert Tearable Pun Here (because paper, ha HA), and is the seventh episode in the series. To quote from the site;

We’re back from a long hiatus! On this episode of YASP, I’m joined by Yijun. We delve into traditional Chinese arts, seal stamp carving, brush calligraphy, and lightly touch on DnD and world-building. We also discuss origami and the his surprisingly large percentage of his budget dedicated high-end paper.

We chatted for just over an hour about all the topics included above, as well as a bit on tech and gaming. There was a story at the start about how I got my current phone, but unfortunately it did not make the edit, due to recording issues. I'll include it below though.

My current phone is a Vivo V15, that I got in 2019. Now Vivo does not sell in this country (Oppo and OnePlus do), and so of course I didn't get it in this country. Before I got my current phone, I had a Samsung C5 (which yes, also cannot be bought in this country, but beside the point). I'm not the most deft with holding a phone, and have dropped it before, onto concrete no less, but it survived a good two+ years. During a trip to Thailand with a friend's family that easter of 2019, I did the thing again, and managed to fumble and drop my phone, however this time the Thai concrete bested it, and *crack*, it was dead.

So I was in trouble. Big trouble. Not having a phone is pretty much a "can't do" now. Thus, we decided to get me a new phone in Bangkok, and I chanced upon the V15, which just released that year. It had no notch (nor bezel), the camera was a pop-up which was a cool but otherwise unimportant feature, had decent specs and cost about £280 in Thai Baht, so I decided to go for it. Thankfully, it's lasted me until today.

I'm rather religious with keeping screen protectors on now.

For more stuff, including revelations on my awful, awful Windows programming setup (I plan to buy an SSD and dual boot linux as soon as possible), trashing on Windows in general, a casual chat about war crimes in Rimworld and Stellaris, and of course origami, seal script, chinese art, and more.
